Manuel Antonio has a reputation for being expensive — and honestly, it's earned. The combination of a world-class national park, Pacific beaches, and proximity to San José means tourist infrastructure has piled up for decades. Hotels charge San José prices for Caribbean views, restaurants add 10% service charges before you sit down, and some tour operators are banking on the fact that you only have three days and no time to compare options.

But here's the thing: the actual adventures — the park, the ocean, the jungle, the wildlife — are still extraordinary and can be done affordably if you know what you're booking. This guide cuts through the noise and gives you 12 experiences ranked by value, from free to splurge.

The Manuel Antonio Reality Check

Manuel Antonio National Park is legitimately one of the most beautiful places in Costa Rica — the combination of jungle, sloth-filled trees, and white-sand beaches is genuinely rare. But the park caps daily visitors at 600 people, which means you must book in advance. This is non-negotiable in high season (December–April). Walk-ups are turned away daily.

Quick fact: Manuel Antonio is the smallest national park in Costa Rica but historically one of its most visited. The visitor cap was introduced to protect the ecosystem — book your park entry at least 48 hours ahead through SINAC (the official system) or through a vetted tour operator.

The town of Quepos, 7km north of the park, is your budget base. Hotels and restaurants here are significantly cheaper than the park road strip — and the 20-minute local bus between Quepos and Manuel Antonio runs every 30 minutes for under $1.

All 12 Adventures, Ranked by Value

  • 01

    Manuel Antonio National Park (self-guided)

    Four beaches, monkey-filled trees, sloths, iguanas, and more birds than you can count. The best bang-for-buck in all of Costa Rica. Go early, go slow, bring binoculars.

    $18 entry Half or full day Easy
  • 02

    Guided wildlife walk in the park

    Hire a certified naturalist guide at the park entrance for $25–30. They carry telescopic lenses and find the sloths, poison dart frogs, and basilisk lizards you'd walk straight past. Worth every colón.

    From $25/person 3–4 hours Easy
  • 03

    Sea kayaking & snorkeling tour

    Paddle along the park's coastline and snorkel the rocky outcrops just offshore. Sergeant major fish, pufferfish, and the occasional reef shark. Equipment and guide included.

    From $55/person 3 hours Easy–Moderate
  • 04

    Zip line canopy tour

    10-cable circuit through the forest above the park. Views stretch to the Pacific on clear days. One of the better canopy setups on the Pacific coast — cables are long and well-maintained.

    From $60/person 2.5 hours Moderate
  • 05

    Sunset sailing cruise

    Catamaran along the coast at golden hour, with snorkeling stops, open bar, and a Caribbean-style dinner. Sounds touristy — and it is — but it's genuinely beautiful and a great social experience.

    From $85/person 4 hours Easy
  • 06

    Stand-up paddleboarding (Biesanz Beach)

    Biesanz Beach — accessible only by boat or a 20-minute hike — is calm, sheltered, and rarely crowded. Perfect for first-time paddleboarders. Rent equipment from Quepos operators.

    From $30/person 2 hours Easy
  • 07

    Sport fishing (shared charter)

    Quepos is one of Costa Rica's top sportfishing ports — marlin, sailfish, dorado, and roosterfish are all in season at different times of year. Shared charters make it affordable.

    From $120/person Half or full day Easy
  • 08

    White-water rafting on the Naranjo River

    Class III–IV rapids through canyon jungle. Less famous than Turrialba-area rivers but closer to Manuel Antonio and equally thrilling in wet season. Transport included from Quepos.

    From $75/person Half day Moderate–Hard
  • 09

    ATV jungle tour

    30–40km of trails through farms, rivers, and forest with Pacific ocean viewpoints. Great for groups and couples — most operators offer tandem ATVs for two.

    From $80/person 3 hours Easy–Moderate
  • 10

    Whale watching (July–October)

    Humpback whales breed in the Marino Ballena area just south of Manuel Antonio from July to October. Offshore Quepos also sees dolphins year-round. Morning departures give the best odds.

    From $65/person 3 hours Easy
  • 11

    Surf lesson (Playa Bejuco or Playa Hermosa)

    Manuel Antonio's beaches aren't ideal for learning surf — too rocky, too crowded. Drive 30 minutes south to Playa Bejuco or Hermosa for long beach breaks and proper beginner conditions.

    From $45/person 2 hours Easy
  • 12

    Night wildlife tour

    Manuel Antonio transforms at night — red-eyed tree frogs, sleeping toucans, caimans, and enormous insects come out after dark. A guided night walk around the park perimeter costs a fraction of daytime tours.

    From $35/person 2 hours Easy

What to Skip in Manuel Antonio

  • Overpriced ATV "combos." Some operators bundle zip line + ATV + waterfall into a $200 day package. These are always rushed and use older equipment. Book each activity separately.
  • Swimming at Playa Espadilla (outside the park). Beautiful beach, but strong rip currents. The beaches inside the park — Playa Manuel Antonio and Biesanz — are far safer.
  • Private park guides who approach you at the entrance. Licensed guides wear SINAC-certified badges. Anyone approaching you outside the gate is unlicensed — skip them.
  • The "wildlife sanctuary" near the park. There are legitimate rescue centers in Costa Rica — this is not one of them. Save your money.
