About this experience

Walk through one of Costa Rica's most famous national parks with a deeply knowledgeable bilingual guide. Manuel Antonio National Park is renowned for its extraordinary biodiversity — home to sloths, three species of monkeys, reptiles, hundreds of bird species and some of the most beautiful beaches on the Pacific Coast.

Your guides come equipped with Pentax and Swarovski telescopes so you can observe wildlife up close and capture incredible photos. Halfway through the hike, you'll enjoy a relaxing 20-minute stop at the world-famous Third Beach — perfect for a swim or just taking in the scenery.

Day itinerary

1

Hotel pickup
Your guide picks you up at your hotel in Quepos or Manuel Antonio. During the drive, they'll give you an introduction to the park and what to expect.
2
~7:30 AM
Park entrance & briefing
Arrive at the park entrance. Your guide briefs the group on rules, wildlife observation etiquette and likely species.
3
7:30 AM – 10:00 AM
Guided nature hike
Walk the flat 2-mile trail through primary and secondary forest. Guide stops frequently with the telescope — sloths, monkeys, birds and reptiles are commonly spotted.
4
~10:00 AM
Third Beach stop (20 min)
20-minute stop at the world-famous Third Beach — one of the most beautiful in Costa Rica. Optional swim or simply relax.
5

Continue the loop back through the forest with more wildlife spotting opportunities.
6
~10:30 AM
Hotel drop-off
Transportation back to your hotel. Tour concludes approximately 3.5 hours after departure.
