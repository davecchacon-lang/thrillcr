Every year, millions of tourists book their Costa Rica activities through Viator or GetYourGuide — and there's nothing wrong with that. Both platforms are legitimate, have real reviews, and provide real booking protection. The question is whether you're getting good value, and whether the convenience justifies the premium.

We built ThrillCR because we saw this gap every day: tourists paying $120 on Viator for an experience that local operators offered for $75, while the local operator received only $65 of that $120. The OTA (Online Travel Agency) model extracts value from both ends. This guide gives you the numbers so you can decide.

How Viator Actually Works

Viator, owned by TripAdvisor since 2014, operates as a marketplace that charges operators a commission of 20–30% on every booking. Operators who want better placement in search results pay even more. To protect their margins, most operators either inflate list prices to absorb the commission, or they offer Viator a stripped-down version of their tour with fewer inclusions.

This creates what's known in the industry as "Viator pricing" — the same activity, same guide, same river, but 20–25% higher sticker price, sometimes with lunch removed or group sizes increased.

What you don't see on Viator: Most Costa Rica operators who list on Viator also offer the same activity direct — often with more flexibility, smaller group sizes, and better inclusions. Viator's terms of service technically prohibit operators from advertising lower prices elsewhere, but this is rarely enforced for small Costa Rican operators who sell direct to walk-ins.

GetYourGuide operates similarly, with commissions in the 20–25% range. Both platforms invest heavily in Google advertising to appear above local operators in search results — which is why you often find them first, not because they're better, but because they outbid everyone else for keywords.

When Viator IS Worth Using

We're being honest here — there are genuine situations where Viator or GetYourGuide is the right call:

  • You're visiting a very remote area where local operators have no English-language web presence and you can't phone ahead. For some Caribbean-side activities or remote Osa Peninsula experiences, Viator may be the only way to book in advance.
  • Last-minute bookings when operators are sold out. Viator's inventory sometimes includes allocations that operators hold back from their own direct booking systems for peak days.
  • You need Viator's dispute resolution process. If something goes wrong, Viator's customer service is responsive and their refund policy is reliable. If you book directly with a small operator and they cancel on you the morning of, recovery can be harder.
  • You're combining many destinations and want one platform. If your trip covers Costa Rica, Panama, and Colombia, having everything in one Viator account simplifies logistics.
Pro move: Use Viator for research — its review volume makes it genuinely useful for identifying which operators are reputable in a given area. Then take the operator name from the Viator listing, find their direct website or WhatsApp, and book direct or through ThrillCR for a better price.

Is Booking Direct Safe in Costa Rica?

This is the real question behind most Viator vs. direct debates, and it deserves a direct answer: yes, if you book through vetted channels.

Costa Rica has strong adventure tourism regulation through ICT (the Instituto Costarricense de Turismo) and SINAC for national park activities. Certified operators are registered, insured, and their guides hold valid certifications from bodies like the Cruz Roja and ICT. The problem is that none of this is visible to a tourist browsing Viator — and it's equally invisible when booking an unlicensed operator direct.

The cases where direct booking goes wrong in Costa Rica typically involve:

  • Unlicensed guides soliciting tourists at park entrances (always skip these)
  • Operators with no insurance who disappear after a cancellation
  • Cash-only operators who have no accountability when equipment fails

None of these risks are solved by Viator — Viator does not verify guide certifications or insurance. What Viator provides is review accountability and dispute resolution. Final Verdict

For most Costa Rica activities, the optimal booking order is:

  1. ThrillCR — vetted operators, transparent pricing, local knowledge, lower commission means better prices
  2. Operator direct — lowest price, maximum flexibility, but requires more research to verify legitimacy
  3. GetYourGuide — slightly lower commissions than Viator, good for research

The bottom line: every dollar you don't spend on OTA commissions either stays in your pocket or goes to the local guide who's actually showing you the river, the jungle, or the volcano. That feels like the right outcome.
