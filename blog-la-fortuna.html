La Fortuna is Costa Rica's adventure capital — and it's not even close. Anchored by the perfectly conical Arenal Volcano, this small town in the northern lowlands packs more adrenaline per square kilometer than almost anywhere else on earth. Waterfalls, rivers, zip lines, hot springs, wildlife, and canyons all within a 30-minute drive. If you only have one week in Costa Rica, make sure at least three nights are spent here.

This guide covers everything: what to do, when to go, how to get there, and exactly which experiences are worth your money.

Why La Fortuna?

Most Costa Rica destinations specialize in one thing — beach towns for surf, cloud forests for birds, Caribbean coast for culture. La Fortuna is the exception. The combination of a active volcano, a major river system (the Sarapiquí and Peñas Blancas), and the enormous Lake Arenal right next door creates a geography that supports almost every adventure sport imaginable.

Local tip: La Fortuna town itself sits safely at the base of the volcano — Arenal last erupted significantly in 2010, and the area is continuously monitored by OVSICORI (Costa Rica's volcano observatory). The hike up to the old lava fields is genuinely dramatic and 100% safe on guided tours.

The town is also refreshingly authentic. Unlike Manuel Antonio or Jacó, La Fortuna hasn't been completely taken over by tourist infrastructure. You'll find family-run sodas (local restaurants), affordable hostels, and guides who have worked these rivers and trails for decades.

Which activity should you prioritize?

If you only do one thing in La Fortuna, do the white-water rafting on the Balsa River. The combination of genuine Class III–IV rapids, pristine jungle scenery, and excellent local guides makes it the best value adventure in the region. The waterfall is a close second — but be warned, the 500-step descent is real and the return climb will humble you.

Best Time to Visit La Fortuna

La Fortuna is in Costa Rica's "Green Zone," meaning it rains year-round — but that's not a bad thing. The rain keeps the rivers full, the waterfalls thundering, and the jungle an impossibly deep green. The real question is how much rain, and when.

Getting to La Fortuna

From San José (3 hours)

The standard route is Highway 27 west to Ciudad Quesada, then north to La Fortuna. The road is fully paved and easy to drive. Shared shuttle services run daily from San José for around $25–35 per person and are comfortable for most budgets.

From Monteverde (2.5 hours)

The famous "jeep-boat-jeep" transfer crosses Lake Arenal by boat — one of the most scenic journeys in Costa Rica. It costs around $35 and takes 2.5 hours vs. 4+ hours by road. Worth every cent.

From Liberia Airport (2 hours)

Guanacaste visitors should head southeast on Highway 1, then cut inland through Cañas toward La Fortuna. Private transfers are efficient if you're arriving with a group.

Insider Tips

  • Book morning activities. La Fortuna clouds over reliably by noon — if you want Arenal volcano views, be on the trail or the zip line before 10am.
  • Skip the Tabacón hot springs resort. Expensive and crowded. Local guides know free and low-cost hot spring access points along the Tabacón River.
  • Waterfall timing matters. The La Fortuna waterfall is most dramatic from June–August when the Fortuna River is at peak volume. In dry season it's still beautiful but smaller.
  • Layer up at night. Elevation means cooler evenings — bring a light fleece even if you're coming from the beach.
  • Book rafting 48 hours ahead. Most Balsa River operators run small groups (8–12 people) and fill up quickly in high season.
